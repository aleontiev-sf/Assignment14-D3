Health and Poverty

This chart depicts the relationship between excellent health and poverty level for various states. There is a significat negative correlation between these two factors: As the percentage of people in poverty increases, the percentage of people who report to be in excellent health decreases--Washington DC is an exception.

Note that this is not necessarily a causal relationship; i.e., poverty does not cause poor health. Most likely this negative relationship is the result of other health factors and habbits in poorer populations that lead to lower levels of health.

Sources of data
U.S. Census Bureau's American Community Survey
American FactFinder tool
Survey data from the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System
